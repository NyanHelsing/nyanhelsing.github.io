Privacy Policy for Nyan Helsing

Nyan Helsing ("us", "we", or "our") is committed to protecting personal information and the right to privacy. To resolve any questions or concerns about this privacy policy or our practices with regard to personal information, please submit an intake.

This privacy policy describes how we might use your information when:

  • Visiting our website at nyanhelsing.github.io
  • Engaging with us in other related ways ― including on social media, or at events.

In this privacy policy, if we refer to:

  • "Website," we are referring to any website of ours that links to this policy
  • "Services," we are referring to our Website, and other related services, including any communications with us on social media or at events.

1. Information We Collect

In the course of operating our Website, we may collect and process the following information:

  • Personal Information: When you submit intake requests, we may ask for your contact information, including information such as name, company name, address, email address, and telephone number.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect in various ways, including to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain our Website
  • Improve, personalize, and expand our Website
  • Understand and analyze how you use our Website
  • Develop new products, services, features, and functionality
  • Communicate with you, either directly or through one of our partners, including for service, to provide you with updates and other information relating to the Website, and for marketing and promotional purposes
    • Status Updates: We’ll use your email address to send you timely updates on the progress of any requests or submissions you’ve made. This way, you’ll always be in the loop about how things are moving along.
  • Account Notifications: For crucial account-related changes—like password resets, security updates, or confirmations of actions you’ve taken (such as receipts)—we’ll reach out via email. This ensures you’re promptly informed of any important changes that affect your account and its security.
  • Find and prevent fraud

3. Sharing Your Information

We do not share your personal information with third parties except as described in this privacy policy. We may share personal with a third party if we have your consent to do so and if the third party is a service provider has a legitimate need to access this information in order to provide services on your behalf.

3.1 Third-Party Service Providers

We are committed to maintaining the confidentiality of your personal information. Part of this commitment involves sharing your data with trusted third-party service providers who perform essential functions on our behalf:

3.1.1 Deno Deploy

To ensure our services are efficient and responsive, we host our server infrastructure on Deno Deploy, a leading edge computing platform. This setup allows us to process and manage your data with high performance and reliability. Here's what this means for your personal information:

  • Self-Managed Processing: While our server is hosted by Deno Deploy, the processing of your personal data — from intake requests to account changes — is conducted by our own applications. Deno Deploy provides the computing environment, but the management and processing of data are performed under our direct control and oversight of our applications.

3.1.2 Supabase

We utilize Supabase for our database needs, which involves storing personal data such as email addresses, TikTok usernames,and other information you provide. Supabase is committed to data security and privacy, ensuring your information is handled with care and in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

3.1.3 Sentry

To enhance the reliability and performance of our services, we utilize Sentry, an error tracking service that helps us identify and fix issues in real-time. Sentry is designed to improve the user experience by ensuring our application runs smoothly and efficiently.

3.1.3.a Data Handling with Sentry
  • Intentional Data Minimization: We take proactive steps to configure Sentry in a way that minimizes the capture of personal information. Our goal is to capture only the technical details necessary to diagnose and resolve errors, without collecting or storing personal data.
  • Potential for Inadvertent Data Capture: While our intention is to avoid personal data capture in error logs, there are scenarios where personal information might be included within an error message or stack trace. This can happen inadvertently due to the nature of the errors being logged.
3.1.3.b Safeguards and Mitigation Strategies
  • Data Filtering and Masking: We implement data filtering and masking techniques to prevent personal information from being captured by Sentry. This includes configuring Sentry's settings to exclude sensitive data fields and using data scrubbing features to remove any inadvertently captured personal information.
  • Review and Compliance: We regularly review the data captured by Sentry to ensure compliance with our privacy standards and data protection laws. If personal information is identified in an error log, we take immediate steps to remove it and adjust our data capture mechanisms to prevent future occurrences.

4. Retention of Your Information

We will retain your personal information only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this privacy policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law (such as tax, accounting, or other legal requirements).

5. Security of Your Information

We are committed to protecting your information through a system of organizational and technical security measures. We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect the security of any personal information we process.

These measures include:

  • Limiting access to your personal information to only individuals with a legitimate need to know and ensuring that they are subject to duties of confidentiality
  • Procedures for dealing with data breaches (the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, your personal information) including notifying you and/or the regulator where we are legally required to do so
  • Using encryption to secure information during transit

Remember that we cannot guarantee that the internet itself is 100% secure. Although we will do our best to protect your personal information, the transmission of personal information to and from our Website is at your own risk. You should only access the services within a secure environment.

6. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on the laws of your country, you may have the right to request access to the personal information we collect from you, change that information, or delete it in some circumstances. To request to review, update, or delete your personal information, please submit an intake.

7. Updates to This Policy

We're committed to continuously improving our privacy practices and ensuring they reflect the latest in data protection standards. Our Privacy Policy may evolve over time to incorporate new regulatory requirements, technological advancements, and to better serve your needs. Whenever we update our policy, we will take appropriate steps to inform you, such as through a notification on our website or a direct communication, depending on the nature of the changes. We encourage you to review our policy periodically to stay informed about how we are protecting your information. If a change is unacceptable to you, then you should stop using the Website and can request that we remove your personal information.
